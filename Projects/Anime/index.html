Shonen

Literally shonen means "for boys", as the genre was geared towards males but as anime in this genere, such as My Hero Academia, Bleach and DBZ became more popular amoung both genders the genre honen anime encompasses an enormous variety of subgenres: Sports, fantasy, science fiction, and even horror stories are found beneath this umbrella.

Shoujo

As shonen was meant for boys, shoujo were geared towards girls. Shoujo anime emphasizes on relationships romanitc and platonic. This can be seen in popular shoujo animes such as Sailor Moon, Cardcaptor Sakura, Fruits Basket and Nana.

Seinen

This anime translate to "anime for young men". Seinen encompasses a wide variety of shows that portray R-rated levels of violence and sexuality, murkier morals, and characters that are not promised any sort of absolution. they're often about people who tried, failed, and now live in the wreckage of that dream. Although most are like this there are some such as One-Punch Man that diviate from this gloom and doom.

Josei

A grown up version of Shoujo is Josei. It goes beyond the intial love story that is dominant in shoujo. Josei anime and mangas deals with breakups, makeups, and all othe aspects of adult life and relationships.

Ecchi

This genre is known for is curvaceous female characters, its sexy, flity atmosphere and ultimately its character developments, storytelling and humor.
